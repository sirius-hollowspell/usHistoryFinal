Michelle Obama

2009 - 2017

  • She was the first African American First Lady
  • Michelle earned a juris doctor degree from Harvard Law School
  • During her time in the White House she established herself as a strong advocate for women and girls in the U.S. and around the world
  • Started “Let’s Move!” program to end childhood obesity
  • To promote healthy eating, she planted a vegetable garden on the South Lawn of the White House.
  • Lead the “Reach Higher Initiative” to help students understand job opportunities and education and skills they need for those jobs.
  • Launched Let Girls Learn, a U.S. government-wide initiative to help girls around the world go to school and stay in school. As part of this effort, Mrs. Obama is calling on countries across the globe to help educate and empower young women.

Hillary Rodham Clinton

1993 - 2001

  • Graduate of Yale Law School
  • Advised the Children’s Defense Fund
  • Joined impeachment inquiry staff advising the Judiciary Committee of the House of Representatives
  • Served as Arkansas’s First Lady for 12 years
  • Chaired Task Force on National Health Care Reform
  • Leading advocate for expanding health insurance coverage and ensured children were properly immunized
  • She led the fight to pass the Children’s Health Insurance Program
  • Elected United States Senator from New York
  • She is the first First Lady elected to the United States Senate and the first woman elected statewide in New York

Nancy Reagan

1981 - 1989

  • Film and television actress prior to becoming First Lady of California
  • Expanded the Foster Grandparent Program to a national level, to bring together senior citizens and handicapped children
  • Her time in the White House was focused on fighting drugs and alcohol abuse among youth
  • During the 40th Anniversary of The United Nations, she hosted 30 First Ladies for an international drug conference
